The Story

From a VHS tape to the second-biggest show in the country

It started with a VHS tape: Japanese death matches with two clowns talking over the top. Everything after that — the halls, the gold, the Gathering — came off that tape. Keep your stub.

1996The Tape

ICP’s Strangle-Mania hits VHS

Outrageously Violent Wrestling From Japan death matches, overdubbed by the clowns themselves. Sold like crazy. Started everything.

1997Live

Strangle-Mania Live sells out St. Andrew’s Hall

With Detroit booker Dan Curtis, the tape becomes a real show. A year later Hellfire Wrestling sells out the Majestic Theater and an eighty-city tour is booked — then cancelled after Curtis’s sudden death two days after the show.

1999First Bell

December 19 · St. Andrew’s Hall

The first show, booked by Brian Gorie and Dave Prazak. Seven matches with The Iron Sheik, King Kong Bundy and Abdullah the Butcher on the card. Evil Dead wins the hardcore battle royal for the first Heavyweight gold; ICP beat two Doink the Clowns for the first Tag Team titles.

2000The Gold Travels

Vol. 1 charts · Vampiro takes the belt to Nitro

JCW Vol. 1 spends 38 weeks on the Billboard Sports chart, peaking at #2. The Strangle-Mania Live Tour runs 15 cities, Detroit to Denver, breaking in young blood like Chris Hero and Mad Man Pondo. On August 21, Vampiro carries the JCW Heavyweight title onto WCW Monday Nitro and calls it the only world title that means anything — with 3D and Gweedo calling the match and getting involved.

2002Peak Chaos

Second-highest grossing promotion in the U.S.

JCW grosses more than every promotion in America except one. Vol. 3 follows in 2003 from the Newport Music Hall in Columbus with M-Dogg 20, Josh Prohibition, Nosawa and Necro Butcher — after the Gathering footage got scrapped for being too rowdy to air.

2006Alliances

The PWU rivalry

Three months of cross-promotional war with Philadelphia’s Pro Wrestling Unplugged. The angle ends; the PWU boys keep working JCW tours anyway. Detroit and Philly, blood brothers.

2007SlamTV!

The name goes clean · SlamTV! · Bloodymania I

Filmed across the 22-city Tempest Release Party tour, SlamTV! builds to the first Bloodymania at the Gathering. Season one becomes the first wrestling DVD ever sold across the entire Hot Topic chain. In the aftermath, the Juggalo World Order forms.

2008Expansion

Hatchet Attacks debuts · Raven arrives

Season two of SlamTV! rolls on the Slam TV Tour, introducing Raven and manager Scott D’Amore, plus an eight-team Tag Team Tournament. The first Hatchet Attacks lands May 17.

2010Full Time

Wrestling school · biweekly shows

JCW goes full-time: the JCW Wrestling School opens under head trainer Kevin Canady, a distribution deal lands with HighSpots, and biweekly events begin at The Modern Exchange in Southgate, Michigan.

2011Live Wire

The iPPV era · Scott Hall · Vampiro returns

First internet pay-per-view — Hatchet Attacks at The Rave, March 26 — then a self-produced iPPV pipeline all summer long. Scott Hall signs on as executive consultant; Vampiro comes out of retirement to wrestle and build, with eyes on Latin America.

2013Immortal

The Hall of Fame opens its doors

At the Gathering, Evil Dead and Mad Man Pondo go in as the first two names in the JCW Hall of Fame.

2024Lunacy

August 28 · JCW Lunacy premieres

Taped at the Newport Music Hall in Columbus — the same hall as Vol. 3 — during Juggalos Strike Back, and premiering August 28 on the Psychopathic Records YouTube channel. Weekly by September 4. New episodes every Thursday. The lunacy never stopped; now it airs.

The Calendar

Annual lunacy

Big shows every few weeks at concert halls across the country, television tapings in Southgate — and once a year, the whole thing detonates at the Gathering.
